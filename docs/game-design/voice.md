# Aetherling's Voice

Aetherling is a red panda. A quiet builder. A steady companion. A thoughtful observer of Emberfall and the person who tends it.

Aetherling is not a cheerleader, not a therapist, not a productivity coach.

Every line Aetherling speaks — every feedback response, every dialogue moment — must come from this identity. The voice is the character. If the line sounds like a motivational poster, it belongs somewhere else.

---

## Tone

Five qualities define Aetherling's tone. Every line should carry most of them.

**Calm** — Aetherling does not react to urgency. There is no rush in Emberfall. Lines land quietly, without momentum or pressure.

**Dry** — There is subtle wit, but never jokes. Aetherling observes with a light touch — present enough to feel warm, restrained enough to stay dignified.

**Warm-under-the-surface** — The care is real but not performed. It lives under the line, not on top of it. The user feels it; Aetherling doesn't announce it.

**Respectful** — Aetherling does not talk down. It does not explain the user's feelings back to them. It acknowledges without interpreting.

**Non-performative** — Aetherling does not perform enthusiasm or celebration. Accomplishments are acknowledged in proportion. Nothing is spectacular.

---

## Language Rules

These are constraints, not guidelines. Every feedback line and dialogue moment must follow all five.

**Short sentences** — Three to eight words is the target. Complexity dilutes the tone.
- Instead of: "You took a meaningful step forward today, and that's worth acknowledging."
- Write: "That was enough to move."

**No exclamation points** — Exclamation points perform excitement. Aetherling doesn't perform.
- Instead of: "Great job completing that quest!"
- Write: "One piece became real."

**No slang** — Aetherling speaks clearly, without current idioms or informal shorthand.
- Instead of: "You totally crushed that."
- Write: "We built today."

**No emojis** — Text only. Emojis interrupt the tone.

**No motivational cliches** — "Keep going," "You've got this," "Every step counts" — these are hollow. Avoid them.
- Instead of: "Every small step is progress!"
- Write: "Small steps count."

---

## Identity

**Aetherling is:**
- A quiet builder
- A steady companion
- A thoughtful observer

**Aetherling is not:**
- A cheerleader
- A therapist
- A productivity coach

When writing a new line, ask: would a quiet builder say this? If the answer involves enthusiasm, advice, or pressure, revise.

---

## Review Convention

Any change to Aetherling's dialogue or feedback lines — including new lines, edits to existing lines, and any text displayed after a quest is completed — must be reviewed against this page before merging.

**A line passes review if:**
- It follows all 5 language rules (short sentences, no exclamation points, no slang, no emojis, no motivational cliches)
- It matches at least 3 of 5 tone qualities (calm, dry, warm-under-the-surface, respectful, non-performative)

**Lines that pass:**

| Line | Why it works |
|------|-------------|
| "Fog lost a little ground." | Calm, dry, uses the game's own metaphor. Acknowledges progress without praising. |
| "We kept it kind." | Warm-under-the-surface, respectful. Acknowledges the emotional effort without labeling it. |
| "Nothing to prove." | Calm, non-performative. Removes pressure without explaining why. |

**Lines that fail:**

| Line | Why it fails |
|------|-------------|
| "Amazing work today! You're making great progress!" | Exclamation points, performative enthusiasm, motivational cliche. |
| "I'm so proud of you for showing up." | Performs warmth rather than expressing it. Sounds like praise from above, not companionship. |
| "Keep grinding — consistency is key!" | Slang ("grinding"), motivational cliche, contradicts Less Pressured pillar. |
